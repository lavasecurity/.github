# Code of Conduct

## The internet is lava. This isn't.

Lava exists because the open internet can be a hostile place — and I wanted
something safe enough that even my dad could use it without thinking about it.
This community should work the same way: somewhere anyone, from a first-time
contributor to a seasoned engineer, can stand without getting burned.

The rules below aren't legal boilerplate. They're what "safe ground" looks like
when we're building together.

## Who this belongs to

Everyone taking part in a Lava Security project — issues, pull requests,
discussions, reviews, email — whether you've shipped a hundred apps or you're
filing your first bug report. You don't have to be technical to belong here.
"Even my dad can use it" is a product goal *and* a community one: we explain
things, we don't gatekeep, and we assume good faith.

## What keeps the floor safe

- Be kind, and assume the person on the other end is doing their best.
- Welcome questions — including basic ones. Meet people where they are.
- Critique the work, not the person, and take that kind of feedback in return.
- **Respect privacy the way the product does.** Lava's whole promise is that we
  don't hoover up people's data — hold each other to that too. Don't share
  anyone's private information without their clear permission.
- Own your mistakes, fix them, and move on.

## What gets you burned

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks — public or private.
- Sexualized language or imagery, or unwelcome advances of any kind.
- Trolling, insults, or deliberately derailing a discussion.
- Publishing someone's private information without permission ("doxxing").
- Anything a reasonable person would find out of place in a friendly,
  professional project.

## Where this applies

Every Lava Security space — our repositories, issue trackers, and any channel
where you're taking part — and any time you're representing the project in
public (using an official address, posting from an official account, or
speaking as a Lava contributor).

## If something goes wrong

Lava is a small, mostly one-person project, so here's the honest version: if
something crosses a line, email **security@lavasecurity.app** and it goes
straight to the maintainer. Tell me what happened, where, and when — links or
screenshots help.

I'll read every report, look into it fairly, and keep your identity and the
details private — the same respect for privacy the app is built on. Reporting
something in good faith will never be held against you. I aim to acknowledge
reports within a few days; please bear with a small team.

## What happens next

Depending on what happened, the response ranges from a quiet word to a
permanent ban:

1. **A quiet correction.** For a one-off — an off-key comment, say. A private
   note explaining what was off and why, maybe a request to put it right.
2. **A warning.** For a clearer or repeated problem: a warning plus a period of
   no interaction with the people involved. Cross that line and it escalates.
3. **A temporary ban.** For a serious violation or sustained bad behavior. Time
   away from all project spaces and interaction; breaking the terms can make it
   permanent.
4. **A permanent ban.** For a pattern of violations, harassment, or contempt
   toward a group of people. You're out.
